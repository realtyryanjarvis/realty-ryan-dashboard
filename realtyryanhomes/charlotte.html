Charlotte, North Carolina

A city of old oaks and new ambition. Where Southern roots meet modern momentum — and every neighborhood tells a different story.

Explore Neighborhoods

Why Charlotte

Charlotte doesn't ask for your attention — it earns it. The second-largest financial hub in the country, a culinary scene that punches way above its weight, and a quality of life that keeps pulling people from the coasts. Four mild seasons. No state income tax on the horizon debates. An airport that can get you anywhere nonstop.

The metro has added more than 200,000 residents in the past five years, yet Charlotte still feels navigable — a city of distinct neighborhoods, each with its own rhythm. From the century-old canopy of Myers Park to the crane-dotted skyline of South End, from the gallery walks of NoDa to the quiet estates of Eastover, the city offers range that few Sun Belt metros can match. It's a place where a young professional, a growing family, and a downsizing empty-nester can all find exactly what they're looking for — often within a ten-minute drive of each other.

This is Charlotte right now: established enough to trust, dynamic enough to bet on.

The Neighborhoods

Where Charlotte lives

Myers Park

Old Charlotte at its finest

Tree-lined boulevards, brick Georgian estates, and the kind of quiet confidence that comes from a century of good taste. Queens Road winds through like a postcard. Myers Park Traditional, Selwyn Elementary, and the proximity to Freedom Park make this the neighborhood Charlotte families aspire to.

Price Range $750K – $5M+ Schools Myers Park High, Selwyn Elem Vibe Established, elegant, family

Dilworth

Charlotte's most walkable neighborhood

Historic bungalows with deep front porches, a Saturday morning scene at Latta Park, and East Boulevard's stretch of restaurants and coffee shops that make car keys optional. Dilworth has the rare combination of genuine walkability and residential calm that most neighborhoods only promise.

Price Range $600K – $2.5M Schools Dilworth Elementary, Myers Park High Vibe Walkable, historic, community-driven

South End

Where the energy is

The LYNX light rail is the spine, breweries and rooftop bars are the personality, and a steady stream of new high-rises and townhomes keep the skyline changing. South End is Charlotte's engine for young professionals — urban without the friction, social without trying too hard.

Price Range $350K – $1.2M Schools Sedgefield Middle, South Meck High Vibe Urban, social, transit-connected

Sedgefield

South End's quieter neighbor

Right next door to South End but with a residential heartbeat. New construction is blending with midcentury originals, Park Road Shopping Center anchors daily life, and the value proposition here — for now — is one of Charlotte's best-kept secrets. That window is closing.

Price Range $450K – $1.5M Schools Sedgefield Elementary, South Meck High Vibe Up-and-coming, new construction, value

Plaza Midwood

Eclectic. Unapologetic.

Central Avenue is the corridor — taquerias next to craft cocktail bars next to vintage shops. Plaza Midwood is where Charlotte's creative class eats, drinks, and argues about what Charlotte used to be. The homes range from Craftsman bungalows to bold new builds, and the vibe is unmistakably its own.

Price Range $400K – $1.2M Schools Shamrock Gardens Elem, Garinger High Vibe Eclectic, foodie, independent

NoDa

Charlotte's arts district

Galleries, murals, live music, and the kind of breweries where the bartender remembers your order. NoDa — North Davidson — has been Charlotte's creative heart for two decades. The monthly gallery crawl still draws crowds, and the neighborhood has held onto its soul even as investment has poured in.

Price Range $350K – $900K Schools Highland Mill Montessori, North Meck High Vibe Artistic, creative, brewery culture

Villa Heights

Charlotte's hottest infill market

Optimist Hall turned this neighborhood into a destination, and builders followed. Modern new construction on every block, walkable to NoDa's galleries and restaurants, and a pace of transformation that makes last year's comps irrelevant. If you want to be ahead of the curve, this is the curve.

Price Range $500K – $1.1M Schools Villa Heights Elem, Garinger High Vibe New construction, walkable, momentum

Eastover

Ultra-luxury. Ultra-private.

Charlotte's most prestigious residential enclave. Gated estates on sprawling lots, mature hardwoods, and the kind of privacy that money can buy when you know where to spend it. Close to SouthPark's shopping and dining, but a world apart in feel. This is legacy real estate.

Price Range $1.5M – $8M+ Schools Eastover Elementary, Myers Park High Vibe Estate living, private, prestigious

Ballantyne

Suburban luxury, corporate hub

Golf courses, top-rated schools, and a corporate corridor that keeps the talent local. Ballantyne is Charlotte's answer to the family that wants space, polish, and a 15-minute commute. The Ballantyne Hotel, Stonecrest shopping, and a manicured community feel make this south Charlotte's gold standard for suburban living.

Price Range $500K – $2.5M Schools Ardrey Kell High, Community House Middle Vibe Family, golf, corporate, polished

Mammoth Oaks

New luxury in 28270

A boutique luxury subdivision in one of Charlotte's most sought-after zip codes. New construction with the finishes and floorplans that today's buyers actually want, zoned for Providence Day and Ardrey Kell — the school pairing that drives south Charlotte's premium. Limited inventory, high demand.

Price Range $900K – $1.8M Schools Providence Day, Ardrey Kell High Vibe New luxury, elite schools, exclusive

SouthPark

Shopping, dining, refined living

Anchored by SouthPark Mall and the boutique corridor of Phillips Place, this is Charlotte's commercial and social center of gravity for the well-heeled. High-end condos, renovated ranches, and new custom builds share space with some of the city's best restaurants. Central to everything, convenient to everyone.

Price Range $500K – $3M+ Schools Sharon Elementary, South Meck High Vibe Upscale, convenient, dining destination

Cotswold

Central and underrated

Cotswold Village shops give it a neighborhood anchor, the location is unbeatable — central to Uptown, SouthPark, and Plaza Midwood — and the housing stock is an interesting mix of midcentury ranches and contemporary new builds. It's the kind of neighborhood that locals know and newcomers discover second.

Price Range $400K – $1.5M Schools Cotswold Elementary, Myers Park High Vibe Central, village feel, mix of old + new

Uptown

The city center

Panthers, Hornets, Spectrum Center, the epicenter of Charlotte's corporate life — and an increasingly interesting residential play. High-rise condos with skyline views, walkable nightlife, and the pulse of a city that's still figuring out how urban it wants to be. For the buyer who wants to be in the middle of it all.

Price Range $250K – $1.5M Schools First Ward Elem, various magnets Vibe Urban core, nightlife, condos, corporate
