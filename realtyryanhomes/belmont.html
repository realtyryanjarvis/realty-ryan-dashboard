The Area

A Mill Town Reimagined

Belmont was once a quiet textile town on the banks of the Catawba River. Today, it's one of the Charlotte metro's most talked-about destinations ‚Äî a place where historic storefronts have become craft breweries and boutiques, where the greenway connects neighborhoods to the river, and where new families are putting down roots alongside lifelong residents.

Main Street Belmont has undergone a revival that feels organic rather than manufactured. Independent coffee shops, locally owned restaurants, and walkable blocks give the town a character that larger suburbs can't replicate. And at just twenty minutes from Uptown Charlotte, the commute is shorter than most intown routes.

It's no surprise that Belmont has emerged as one of the fastest-growing communities in the region ‚Äî blending affordability, quality of life, and proximity in a way that's hard to find anywhere else.

Neighborhoods

Where to Live in Belmont

From historic cottages to brand-new construction, Belmont's neighborhoods each carry their own personality.

Downtown Belmont

Walkable streets, historic Craftsman and Victorian homes, plus new infill construction. The heart of Belmont ‚Äî where Main Street is your front yard.

South Point

Newer subdivisions with lake access and family-friendly layouts. Growing fast with national and regional builders offering affordable luxury from the mid $300s.

Cramerton

Adjacent to Belmont with the Goat Island greenway, riverfront parks, and a community feel all its own. A hidden gem for those who want to be close but a step quieter.
