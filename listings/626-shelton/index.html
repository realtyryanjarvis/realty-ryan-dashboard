The Arrival

First impressions, elevated.

Natural stone and cream brick frame a façade of quiet authority — charcoal slate gables overhead, black-framed windows catching light. Step through warm wood double doors into an entrance foyer that opens onto a dedicated wine room, setting the tone before you've taken your coat off.

Or slip in through the friends' entry — because the best homes welcome you on your terms.

Transitional architecture · 2 stories · Natural stone + cream brick exterior · Wine room off foyer · Gallery hallway

The Heart

A kitchen that performs.

Twenty-seven feet of Calacatta marble countertops, a chevron herringbone backsplash, and a satin gold bridge faucet over a farmhouse sink. Column refrigerator and column freezer flanking the kitchen. This kitchen doesn't compromise — and neither should you.

The dedicated scullery — nearly 275 square feet of chef's prep kitchen — handles the chaos. The butler's pantry stages the performance. You handle the applause.

The Retreat

Where Saturdays were meant to be spent.

A pool that catches the Carolina sun. A covered porch that holds the evening. An outdoor kitchen built for more than burgers. Nearly half an acre of privacy in one of Charlotte's newest enclaves — close enough to everything, far enough from everyone.

The Primary Suite

On the first floor — because luxury shouldn't require stairs. Eighteen feet of retreat, a freestanding tub, rain shower, polished nickel and brass mixed metals. This is your morning. Designed accordingly.

The Upper Level

Room to grow. Room to play.

A loft that flexes from media room to lounge. A 650-square-foot bonus room stretching the full length above the garage — with a built-in homework station and wet bar, because teenagers and hosting both deserve their own wing.

Three additional bedrooms, each with walk-in closets, give everyone space to breathe.
